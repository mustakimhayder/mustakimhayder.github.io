About Me
Mustakim Haydar
Hello! I'm Md. Mustakim Hayder, a master's student in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology, where I also completed my bachelor's degree. My current research focuses on laser-matter interactions, specifically examining how materials respond to high-intensity lasers with ultra-short pulse durations. I'm particularly intrigued by the structural dynamics of materials at the nanoscale under extreme conditions, which are often encountered in advanced manufacturing processes. This work has led me to develop expertise in microscale computational modeling using molecular dynamics. I'm driven by a relentless pursuit of knowledge, always ready to tackle unfamiliar and challenging topics. This passion for discovery has naturally drawn me towards a research career. My experiences have molded me into a goal-oriented and resilient individual, particularly when facing challenges. I view each obstacle as an opportunity for growth and learning in my ongoing journey of scientific exploration.
Research
Ultrafast laser ablation
When metal are irradiated with laser at high fluence under very short pulse duration, huge amount of energy is generated within surface region. The timescale of energy deposition is so small compared to the time required for heat to be conducted away effectively. As a result the material just below the surface still remains relative cold. But as the super hot material above tends to expand a large compressive shock is generated which rapidly travels along depth and causes a tensile rarefaction to follow along. The fragile state of material at extremely high temperature combined with the superimpose rarefaction causes material to eject away a greater speed. This phenomenon is known as ablation.
